Creating Plant Database for Native Plants on Campus

Project Overview

This project’s objective is to incorporate a Native Hawaiian framework in web design to educate the public about native flora conservation at Kapiʻolani Community College. 

Over 90 native plant pages were created that included cultural and medicinal usages, mythology, and Hawaiian and Latin identification and taxon families. Pages use Hawaiian language headers and categories and are organized according to the Hawaiian alphabet. Design choices were made to reflect Hawaiian values through images, layout, and connecting student stories to the project and plants.

Project Results

The result is a public website that visitors can access via QR code signs. Future steps include creating traditional motifs and expanding to other gardens. Developing a framework for indigenous design is important to preserve culture and diversify scientific knowledge and communication to a broader audience.
